A little more about me

While I was studying Computer Systems Engineering at Veracruz, I got specialized in "Mobile and Web Development Technologies", so that my first experiences where in that field.

At the end of my bachelor studies, I moved to Guanajuato City for working on my thesis project at the Centro de Investigación en Matemáticas (CIMAT) advised by PhD Rogelio Hasimoto Beltrán and PhD Rafael Rivera López. Honestly, this was one of the best experiences of my life, personally and professionally, because I met amazing people and I learned so much from them.

My Thesis project was about a platform for tracking the public transport system in real-time, a flexible model that could work for every mexican city and would lead the citizen to use the bus instead a private car. For this, I had to go deeper in stuff like high performance software design and load balancing, patterns, web services, networks, protocols, data transmission and others. I took some programming classes and I was the teaching assistant of the "Advanced Programming Topics" class focused on networks programming, protocols and data transmission with C programming language. In general, it was a very enriching stage, I recommend this place.

During my stay at CIMAT, I got the chance to work remotely for the Centro Nacional de Supercómputo (CNS) as a junior software developer, advised by PhD Salvador Ruiz Correa, but in 2017 I moved to San Luis Potosí for working in situ. At this time I was working on a mobile crowdsourcing platform based on a Services Oriented Architecture.

Late in 2017, I was assigned to the Youth Innovation Laboratory (You-i Lab) as the lead web software engineer, an interdisciplinary group focused on tech-projects for social good. This is my current job, such a pleasure to collaborate with this awesome team. Here, I have worked on social problems like pollution, air quality, water conservation and public politics based on the citizens opinions and necessities, applying things like data analytics, social engineering and GIS, besides software development of course.

Since 2019, I've been working as an independent consultant for other companies, like the Instituto Nacional de Antropología e Historia (INAH) and a group called CreadoresEdoMex and I've collaborated with some startups and civil associations like Safety Program and Zynergia, A.C.

Areas of interest

Since I got started in Computer Sciences, I've had big passion for the Software Engineering area. We can find there that programming is a good skill, but is not enough to give solution to a complex problem, to build a platform or to work in a team. We need to learn that planning a project and making up the design is the most important part.

The Machine Learning area is quite new for me, but I've found simply amazing the way in we can teach a machine to execute tasks in a similar way than a person, specially those tasks that are difficult o even impossible for us. So, I'm getting deep into this area.

Finally, it's incredible the amount of data available on the net, and even more the things we can learn or discover for different purposes, like social solutions. That's the reason of my interest on Data Analysis. The complement of this is the way we can show information to the society in general, in my opinion it's very important to complement our studies with Data Visualization skills in order to achieve everyone's understanding and, maybe, empower participation.

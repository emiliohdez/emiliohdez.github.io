A little more about me

I'm currently 27 y.o. and I'm from Veracruz, México. I studied Computer Systems Engineering at the Instituto Tecnológico de Veracruz (ITVer) with an specialization in "Mobile and Web Development Technologies", so my background is oriented to programming, database management and computer networks.

Day of my graduation at the ITVer.

At the end of my bachelor studies, I moved to Guanajuato City for working on my thesis project at the Centro de Investigación en Matemáticas (CIMAT), advised by PhD. Rogelio Hasimoto Beltrán and PhD. Rafael Rivera López. Honestly, this was one of the best experiences of my life, personally and professionally, because I met amazing people and I learned so much from them.

My Thesis project was about a platform for tracking the public transport system in real-time, a flexible model that could work for every mexican city and would lead the citizen to use the bus instead a private car. For this, I had to go deeper in stuff like high performance software design and load balancing, patterns, web services, network protocols, data transmission and others. I took some programming classes and I was the teaching assistant of the "Advanced Programming Topics" class, focused on networks programming, protocols and data transmission with C programming language. In general, it was a very enriching stage, I really recommend this place.

Some of my friends at CIMAT (I don't have other picture).

During my stay at CIMAT, I got the chance to work remotely for the Centro Nacional de Supercómputo (CNS) as a junior software developer, advised by PhD. Salvador Ruiz Correa, but in 2017 I moved to San Luis City for working in situ. At this time I was working on a mobile crowdsourcing platform based on a Services Oriented Architecture.

Late in 2017, I was promoted as the lead web software engineer of the Youth Innovation Laboratory (You-i Lab), an interdisciplinary group focused on tech-projects for social good. This is my current job, such a pleasure to collaborate with this awesome team. Here, I have worked on social problems like pollution, air quality, water conservation and public politics based on the citizens opinions and necessities, applying things like data analytics, social engineering and GIS, besides software development of course.

My team at the You-i Lab

Since 2019, I've been working as an independent consultant too, collaborating with other for other companies and organizations like the Instituto Nacional de Antropología e Historia (INAH).
